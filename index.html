About

I am an artist, musician, and audio engineer living in Pittsburgh, PA. I am a graduate of Carnegie Mellon University with a degree in Interactive Digital Media. I have professional training and work experience in human-computer interaction, game design, audio engineering, and composing music for video games, films, and local ensembles. I studied violin and piano performance with esteemed musicians and professors such as Jack Kurutz, Jim Lyon, Chris Wu, Andres Cardenes, and Frederic Chiu. My love for music extends to many disciplines such as performance, composition, reading, improvisation, and collaboration. I enjoy cycling long distances, playing chess, studying Bach, making games, telling stories, and a good laugh.

From 2019 to 2021, I worked as an audio engineer at Schell Games in Pittsburgh. There I designed and implemented audio for several interactive experiences utilizing virtual and augmented reality technology. In college, I worked as a personal contractor in sound recording for the Pittsburgh Digital Recording & Editing Company under the direction of Riccardo Schulz—capturing professional quality recordings of ensembles such as the Pittsburgh Opera and Chatham Baroque. I also worked for the radio station WQED Pittsburgh—helping record and edit performances by visiting musicians, in addition to writing scripts for Jim Cunningham’s show Voice of the Arts.
